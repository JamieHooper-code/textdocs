general rules- only softcovers, in good condition, no special binding, no nudity, english or spanish only


genres we always need change but occult, western, sci-fi and the like, dictionaries/thesaurus, manga, criminal law, almanacs, job type topics - k


Dictionaries & Thesauruses – Many incarcerated people are trying to improve their vocabulary, write letters, or study legal materials.

Self-Improvement & Skill-Building – Books on trades (carpentry, plumbing, coding, etc.), personal finance, or emotional resilience.

Legal Books – Especially books on prisoners' rights, self-representation, or understanding the legal system.

History & Political Theory – Many people in prison are interested in learning about social justice, civil rights, and revolutionary movements.

Religious & Spiritual Texts – Across different beliefs, books on spirituality, meditation, and philosophy are often requested.

Fantasy & Sci-Fi – Escapism is important, and popular authors include J.R.R. Tolkien, Octavia Butler, and Isaac Asimov.

Classic Literature – Orwell, Steinbeck, Baldwin, Dostoevsky, and other thought-provoking authors.
True Crime & Mystery – Some prisons restrict true crime, but detective novels and mysteries are usually welcome.

Graphic Novels & Comic Books – If allowed, these can be a great source of entertainment and inspiration.
