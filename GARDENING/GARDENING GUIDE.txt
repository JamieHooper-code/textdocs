  │ Notes:        Sandy FL soil needs extra compost;    │

  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Planting from store-bought root:
1. Pick root -- firm, plump, with visible "eyes" (buds); avoid shriveled or moldy
2. Soak in room-temp water 8-24 hrs (removes growth inhibitors on non-organic)
3. Cut into 1-2 inch pieces, each with at least one eye
4. Dry cut pieces 24-48 hrs before planting -- skipping this causes rot
5. Optional: pre-sprout in warm humid spot until small green shoots appear
6. Plant 1-2 inches deep, buds facing up, rich well-draining soil
7. Pot vs. ground: pots = easier control, smaller harvest; ground = bigger harvest

Care notes:
- Partial shade is important -- too much direct sun scorches leaves
- Keep soil consistently moist but never waterlogged
- Sandy soil dries fast -- add compost and mulch around base
- Spreads slowly underground; give it space

What to watch for:
- No shoots after 3-4 weeks = check depth, moisture, and soil temp (needs 70F+)
- Yellow leaves = overwatering or too much direct sun
- Mushy stem at soil line = rot from overwatering or planting before cut dried
- Healthy sign: upright green shoots emerging from soil

SECTION 3: WATERING GUIDE
=======================================================

HOW TO CHECK SOIL BEFORE WATERING
-----------------------------------
1. Push your finger 2 inches into the soil near the base of the plant
2. Dry at that depth: water now
3. Slightly cool/damp: wait a day and check again
4. Still wet: skip -- do not water

For sandy Gainesville soil:
- Dries faster than clay/loam -- check more often in summer heat
- After rain: always check before defaulting to your schedule
- Mulching around base (1-2 inch layer) significantly slows drying

WATERING TECHNIQUE
-------------------
- Water at BASE of plant, not on leaves (wet leaves invite fungal issues)
- Water slowly and deeply -- let it soak in vs. quick surface spray
- Best time: early morning (before 10am) -- reduces evaporation and fungal risk
- Avoid watering in harsh afternoon sun

SIGNS OF UNDERWATERING
------------------------
- Wilting/drooping in morning (not just afternoon heat)
- Dry, crispy leaf edges
- Soil pulling away from pot edges
- Leaves curling inward

SIGNS OF OVERWATERING
-----------------------
- Yellow leaves (especially lower leaves first)
- Soft, dark, or mushy stems at soil line
- Soggy soil that stays wet for days
- Mold on soil surface
- Root rot smell

AFTER RAIN (Gainesville summer storms)
----------------------------------------
- Skip scheduled watering -- check soil first
- Florida afternoon storms are often intense but brief; check if water actually soaked in


=======================================================
SECTION 4: PRUNING GUIDE
=======================================================

GENERAL RULES
--------------
- Prune in the morning -- cooler temps reduce stress
- Never remove more than 1/4 of a plant at once (especially young trees)
- Use clean, sharp tools -- wipe with rubbing alcohol between plants
- After pruning trees: thin mulch layer at base to retain moisture
- Avoid pruning when rain is expected within 24 hours (open cuts + moisture = disease)

WHAT TO REMOVE
---------------
- Dead or dying branches (brown, brittle, no buds)
- Crossing branches (rub against each other, create wounds)
- Inward-growing branches (crowd center, block airflow)
- Suckers (growth from base of tree -- drains energy)
- Water sprouts (fast vertical shoots from main branches)
